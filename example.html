        Atmospheric Reference

        SCENTS OF RAVENWOOD

        Woodsmoke, pine, tobacco, leather, salt, rain on fresh-cut timber, honey, peat moss on the moors.

        PROLOGUE

        "The Lord said, let there be Light. And Darkness became shadow, a brooding god."

        TONE PILLARS

        Beauty and decay are inseparable. Roses bloom against crumbling walls, candlelight dances on rusted iron, knowledge itself may wound as much as it heals.

        The horror comes from the uncertainty of whether the threat is real or imagined.

        Victories are fragile and often incomplete, leaving lingering unease.
